## 1. Organization Overview

BlueDot Impact is a non-profit talent accelerator based in London and San Francisco. Their one-liner: "We help you have a positive impact on the trajectory of AI."

They run free cohort-based courses, help people land jobs, organize events globally, give out grants, and accelerate entrepreneurs to start new companies. Since 2022 they've trained 7,000+ professionals worldwide, from technical staff at frontier AI labs to government policymakers. About 1,000 graduates now work in high-impact roles at organizations including Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and the UK AI Security Institute.

They are a small team (~7 people as of early 2026, scaling to 20-25), and have raised $35M to date. They are currently expanding from London to San Francisco (Q2 2026).

**Brands:** BlueDot Impact (parent org), AI Safety Fundamentals (aisafetyfundamentals.com), Biosecurity Fundamentals. All consolidated under bluedot.org as of May 2025.

- Source: [bluedot.org](https://bluedot.org/), [About page](https://bluedot.org/about)

## 2. Theory of Impact

BlueDot's theory of change has three core pillars, as laid out in their [2023 Impact Report](https://bluedot.org/blog/2023-impact-report) (Dewi Erwan, March 2024):

### Find great people
Their brands aim to attract the most motivated, thoughtful, and action-driven people globally. All courses are part-time (~5 hours/week for 2-3 months) because they expect the best participants to already be in demanding roles.

### Motivate and equip
Courses create a space for participants to evaluate arguments for why a topic matters, equip them with frameworks to assess risks and interventions, and provide an overview of the current landscape. Participants are grouped into small cohorts (5-8 people) with a trained facilitator. Cohort discussions clarify uncertainties, develop relationships, and serve as accountability mechanisms.

### Connect to opportunities
Impact relies on alumni taking action. At the end of each course, students consider what actions they could take. BlueDot works with recruiters at partner organizations to identify promising leads from alumni for open roles.

### Expanded model (2025-2026)
Beyond courses, they've added:
- **Grantmaking**: Rapid Grants (up to $5k), AGI Strategy Fund ($200k+ across 9 grants), and "The Bridge" career transition grants (in development)
- **Startup incubation**: Incubator Week, Startup Studio model
- **Community events**: Global events, reading clubs, meetups
- **Placements pipeline**: Active talent matching between alumni and hiring orgs

### The workforce gap framing
Their overarching argument: "The gap between how fast AI is advancing and how fast the safety and security workforce is growing is the most dangerous mismatch of our time." They estimate ~2,000 people currently work full-time on AI safety, and this needs to reach 100,000 by 2030.

### Key evidence
Their 2022 AI Alignment Course took participants from 5% working on AI safety pre-course to 37% working full-time on AI safety afterward (18 of 342 pre-course, 123 of 342 post-course).

- Source: [2023 Impact Report](https://bluedot.org/blog/2023-impact-report), [2022 Course Impact](https://bluedot.org/blog/2022-ai-alignment-course-impact/)

## 3. Core Beliefs and Values

### Three core beliefs (from the About page)

1. **"AGI could arrive soon, and society is dangerously unprepared."** Human-level AI is likely years away, not decades. AI companies are moving at a blistering pace, while the institutions meant to govern AI are moving at a fraction of the speed required.

2. **"People at key moments rewrite history."** Throughout history, a small number of people in the right positions at the right time have steered powerful technologies toward better outcomes. BlueDot finds and prepares those people.

3. **"We need urgency and wisdom."** The stakes are vast and time is short, but panic and fatalism lead to bad decisions. They advocate thoughtful and rapid action.

### Three values

1. **Own the mission:** Take responsibility for achieving the mission, not just getting tasks done. If something important needs doing and it's not anyone's job, it's your job.
2. **Think hard and move fast:** Think carefully about what matters most, then pursue it with urgency. Speed in the wrong direction is wasted effort.
3. **Say the uncomfortable truth:** Give feedback directly and receive it generously. The mission is too important for politeness to get in the way of honesty.

### What makes BlueDot events different

1. **Structured, not just social.** Networking events use personalized 1-on-1 matching based on registration data, not random mingling.
2. **Cohort foundation.** Community starts with a shared 8-13 week course experience. Events build on pre-existing relationships.
3. **Career-oriented.** Events explicitly aim to create career transitions, not just intellectual exchange.
4. **Empowering community-led hosting.** They train and empower community members to be hosts (e.g., the Women in AI Safety event template requires <2 hours of planning).
5. **Data-driven iteration.** Extensive retrospectives with quantitative data. The June 2024 course retrospective tracked project completion (50%, up from 36%) and quality (56% high-quality, up from 42%).
6. **Co-hosting model.** They partner with complementary orgs rather than competing.
7. **Global + local hybrid.** Virtual recurring groups anyone can join, plus in-person socials in key cities.

### Deep dive: Women in AI Safety events

### Hiring needs research (Feb 2026)
Li-Lian Ang published ["We asked 10+ AI safety orgs about their hiring needs"](https://blog.bluedot.org/p/we-asked-ai-safety-orgs-about-their-hiring-needs) with detailed findings:

**Core challenge:** Orgs need people who can **own and lead work independently** and **care deeply about AI risk**. People with a strong track record who can "hit the ground running."

**What specific profiles look like:**
- **Engineering (Lead/IC):** Technical depth for architectural decisions on complex systems. Shipped high-quality code in demanding environments. Can turn vague requirements into working systems.
- **Research Lead:** End-to-end high-quality research (scoping, methodology, execution, communication). Recognized domain expert with publications.
- **Communications Lead:** Track record of audience-tailored content. Managed large project budgets. Engaged seriously with AI safety content.
- **Policy Lead:** Direct government experience. Strong policy network. Translates technical research into actionable policy memos.

**Key insight:** You don't need years of AI safety experience, but you do need to care. Since few people have 5+ years of AI safety experience, orgs look for strong track records in similar work outside the field. Mission alignment bridges the gap. These are small (<50 person), mission-driven orgs.

**How to get started:** BlueDot's AGI Strategy course is positioned as the entry point. "It's a free, 30-hour course that AI safety orgs view as a strong signal."

### Rapid Grants details (Nov 2025)
Per [Joshua Landes' blog post](https://bluedot.org/blog/rapid-grants-for-bluedot-projects): Grants between $50 and $1,500 for course participants/facilitators. ## 13. Gaps and Opportunities

### What's missing in the SF AI safety scene

1. **No dedicated AI safety talent-matching or career event.** The closest model is the Zurich AI Safety Day's "Field Building & Careers" track. This is a major gap given that BlueDot's mission is explicitly about career transitions.

2. **Coefficient Giving is actively seeking more capacity-building work.** Their [March 2026 EA Forum post](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rAqKSSXankvys2Fzu/) ("The case for AI safety capacity-building work") argues that many marginal hires at safety orgs would have *more* impact in capacity-building roles. They gave away >$150M in 2025 and perceive a shortage of capacity-building interest relative to funding appetite. The capacity-building team (11 staff) is one of three major AI risk teams at Coefficient. They fund MATS, BlueDot, Constellation, 80,000 Hours, CEA, The Curve, FAR.AI events, university groups. Endorsements from Neel Nanda (DeepMind), Marius Hobbhahn (Apollo Research CEO), Ryan Kidd (MATS Director), Buck Shlegeris (Redwood CEO).

3. **SF needs an anchor community institution.** Mox provides space but is still financially fragile (~$30k/mo deficit). BlueDot could become the programming and community backbone for SF AI safety alongside Mox's physical infrastructure.

4. **Cross-pollination between technical and governance communities.** Most events are either technical (reading clubs, hackathons) or policy-focused. Few bridge the two communities in a structured way.

5. **Structured networking over casual socializing.** BlueDot's data-driven matching model (personalized 1-on-1s based on registration data) is differentiated from the typical "show up and mingle" format. This could be a major value-add for an SF event series.

6. **Founder-to-funder connections.** BlueDot is increasingly focused on startups ("AI Safety Needs Startups"), but there's no regular event connecting AI safety founders with funders in SF.

### What BlueDot specifically cares about for events (from role description and org context)

- **Specific outcome, not generic networking.** The work test explicitly says "rather than generic networking, we want you to choose a specific outcome."
- **Repeatable systems.** They value Airtable, n8n, and automation-first thinking.
- **Empowering community members.** Their model is to train hosts, not centrally control everything.
- **Data-driven improvement.** They want systems that help the event "improve month after month."
- **Career acceleration.** Everything ties back to "propelling people into impactful AI safety careers."

### Structured event formats worth noting

- **Speed networking:** Timed 3-8 minute rotations with prompted questions. Prevents clique formation. BlueDot's Women in AI Safety events already use this (7-min speed friending).
- **Unconference / Open Space:** Participants propose and vote on session topics on the day. Common at EA/rationality events.
- **Research sprints:** Apart Research's model (2-3 day hackathons). Scalable (500-766 signups). They publish a [guide on organizing research hackathons](https://apartresearch.com/news/how-to-organize-a-research-hackathon).
- **Academic salon:** Bay Area AI Safety Meetup's bi-monthly format with invited speakers + discussion.
- **Career track:** Zurich AI Safety Day had a dedicated "Field Building & Careers" track covering talent pipelines and funding pathways, the closest model to a safety career event.
