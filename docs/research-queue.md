# Research Queue - BlueDot Event Design Brainstorming

## 1. EAG Events Model
**What:** How Effective Altruism Global events run their 1-on-1 scheduling system, what makes them high-impact, overall format and structure.
**Why:** EAG is the gold standard for high-impact networking in this space. Their system of mass 1-on-1 scheduling drives career outcomes. Understanding what works (and what doesn't) gives us a proven foundation to build on or differentiate from.
**Where to look:** EA Forum retrospectives, CEA blog, attendee write-ups on LessWrong

## 2. AI Safety Hackathons / Apart Research Sprint Model
**What:** How Apart Research runs their monthly research sprints, scaling mechanics (500-766 signups), satellite event model, co-hosting approach.
**Why:** Apart already co-hosts with BlueDot. Their model shows how to create tangible output (research entries) from time-bounded events. Relevant for understanding what "specific outcome" events look like at scale.
**Where to look:** Apart Research blog, their hackathon guide, EA Forum posts about sprint outcomes

## 3. Control Conference
**What:** Format, structure, outcomes, attendance model.
**Why:** A recent event in the AI safety space that could offer learnings on what formats work for technical community gathering.
**Where to look:** Event pages, EA Forum, attendee write-ups

## 4. Swapcard Platform
**What:** Feature set, how EAG uses it for attendee matching, profile building, 1-on-1 scheduling, feedback collection, follow-up tracking.
**Why:** This is the closest existing model to the "system" we need to design for Part 2. Understanding what Swapcard does well (and where it falls short) informs what to build. Eitu's vision: profile building, 1-on-1 scheduling, feedback, follow-ups, funding applications.
**Where to look:** Swapcard product pages, EAG attendee experiences, competitor platforms

## 5. AI Safety Talent Pipeline Bottlenecks
**What:** Where people actually get stuck transitioning into AI safety careers. Which stages of the pipeline leak the most talent. What interventions have been tried and what evidence exists.
**Why:** This is the strategic foundation. The event should target a specific, real bottleneck, not a hypothetical one. BlueDot's own framing is ~2,000 people in safety now, need 100,000 by 2030. Where exactly is the gap?
**Where to look:** BlueDot blog posts (especially Li-Lian Ang's hiring needs research, Dewi's impact reports), EA Forum (Coefficient Giving's capacity-building post), LessWrong, Alignment Forum, 80,000 Hours career guides

## 6. Structured Career-Matching Event Formats
**What:** Models from tech and other fields that go beyond generic networking to produce specific career outcomes. Demo days, reverse job fairs, talent showcases, structured matching events.
**Why:** The assignment explicitly says "rather than generic networking, choose a specific outcome." We need proven formats where putting 50 people in a room for 3 hours reliably produces career movement, not just good vibes.
**Where to look:** YC Demo Day format, Recurse Center career events, tech talent showcase models, hiring-focused meetup formats
