Evidence: Where the Pipeline Breaks

  • Hiring managers need people who can "own and lead work independently" (Li-Lian Ang, 10+ org interviews, Feb 2026)
  • Coefficient Giving reports a shortage of capacity-building applications, not a shortage of funding (Bergal, EA Forum, Mar 2026)
  • 10-30% of fellowship alumni cycle into another fellowship rather than converting to employment (Clay dataset, 600+ alumni, 2026)
  • Senior talent scarcity blocks the whole pipeline: can't mentor juniors without seniors (Teichman, 23 interviews, Mar 2026)

My Approach

I spent most of my time on problem diagnosis before designing anything.

  • Researched the talent pipeline to identify the specific bottleneck
  • Studied existing models: EAG, Apart Research, ControlConf, HackerX, EA North
  • The bottleneck is the transition from training to first impactful role or project
  • Designed the event to address that, then built the system to measure whether it works

The System: 5 Layers

Every event generates data. That data makes the next event better.

LAYER 1
Operations
Swapcard + Luma
LAYER 2
Data Collection
Forms + Surveys
LAYER 3
Participant Tracking
Facilitator calls
LAYER 4
Outcome Tracking
Airtable (live)
LAYER 5
Improvement Engine
Monthly retros

Layer 3 is the differentiator. Every participant gets an assigned facilitator who does monthly calls to convert connections into outcomes. The event alone doesn't produce results. Dedicated follow-up does.

What We Measure

Real outcomes, not just survey scores.

North Star (Tier 1)

Tracked via facilitator calls, not surveys:

  • Job interviews and hires
  • Funding applications submitted
  • Projects and collaborations started
  • Program placements (MATS, etc.)

Leading Indicators (Tier 2-3)

Post-event survey (EAG framework):

  • Plan changes made
  • Counterfactual attribution
  • Meaningful connections per person
  • Likelihood to Recommend (1-10)
