# Loom Video Script

Navigate slides with arrow keys. Each section below matches one slide exactly.

---

## Slide 1: Curated Talent Marketplace
*Show title slide briefly, start talking immediately.*

"Hi, I'm Eitan. For this work test I designed a curated talent marketplace for AI safety. Let me walk you through what I built and why."

---

## Slide 2: The Problem
*Slide shows: 3 stats (7,000+ alumni, 10-30% cycling, $150M+ funding)*

"Before picking an event format, I spent most of my time on problem diagnosis. BlueDot has trained over 7,000 people. Some are genuinely exceptional. But there's no fast track to get them into impactful work. 10-30% of AI safety fellowship alumni cycle into another fellowship rather than getting a job or starting a project. Meanwhile, funders like Coefficient Giving gave away over $150M in 2025 and want more good applications. The talent is there. The opportunities are there. They're just not meeting."

---

## Slide 3: Evidence: Where the Pipeline Breaks
*Slide shows: 4 sourced bullet points*

"The evidence comes from multiple sources. Li-Lian Ang interviewed 10+ hiring managers and found they need people who can own work independently. Coefficient Giving published on the EA Forum that they have a shortage of applications, not funding. Chris Clay's dataset of 600+ fellowship alumni shows 10-30% cycling. And the Teichman study found that senior talent scarcity blocks the whole pipeline. These aren't opinions, they're documented patterns."

---

## Slide 4: My Approach
*Slide shows: 4 bullet points about the research process*

"My approach was to figure out where the pipeline is actually breaking before designing anything. I researched existing models: EAG, Apart Research, ControlConf, HackerX, EA North. The bottleneck is the transition from training to first impactful role or project. So I designed the event to address that specifically, and built a system to measure whether it works."

---

## Slide 5: The Event
*Slide shows: two cards (Institutional Leaders + Top Talent)*

"The event is monthly, invite-only. 50 people, 3 hours. Two sides of the room: 10-15 institutional leaders, funders, hiring managers, program directors. And 30-35 of BlueDot's highest-rated alumni, invited based on course performance and facilitator recommendations."

---

## Slide 6: Why BlueDot
*Slide shows: two cards (Talent data + Demand-side relationships)*

"This requires two things in the same organization. First, talent data: BlueDot has performance data on 7,000+ alumni, enough signal to curate the invite list. Second, demand-side relationships: BlueDot already works with Coefficient Giving, MATS, Apart, Constellation, and hiring managers at safety orgs. Having both is what makes this possible."

---

## Slide 7: The Format
*Slide shows: timeline + 3 stats ($12-28, 10 conversations, 15 min)*

"The format is simple. 10 structured 1-on-1s, 15 minutes each. First five are pre-matched based on profiles. Last five are self-selected. Last 20 minutes, everyone writes down their concrete next steps. Cost is about $12-28 per attendee. We're not reinventing the wheel. EAG proves this model drives career outcomes. We just make it higher signal and monthly."

---

## Slide 8: The System (5 Layers)
*Slide shows: 5 layer cards with Layer 3 highlighted. Switch to Airtable tab here.*

"For Part 2, I built a 5-layer system. The key is Layer 3: participant tracking. Every attendee gets an assigned facilitator who does monthly calls to help them convert connections into real outcomes. The event alone doesn't produce results, dedicated follow-up does. I built a live Airtable base with 6 tables that tracks events, next steps, feedback, outcomes, and individual participant progress. [Switch to Airtable briefly to show it.]"

---

## Slide 9: What We Measure
*Slide shows: two cards (North Star outcomes + Leading Indicators)*

"We measure real outcomes, not just satisfaction scores. The north star is job interviews, hires, funding applications, and collaborations, all traced back to event connections through facilitator calls. There's evidence from an EAGx Australia case-control study that standard surveys alone don't show statistically significant impact at 6 months. That's why the facilitator tracking matters. It's the measurement mechanism that surveys can't replace."

---

## Slide 10: Summary
*Slide shows: 4 stats + 3 evidence-backed summary bullets*

"To sum up. 50 people, 3 hours, monthly, $12-28 per attendee. EAG proves that 1-on-1 matching drives career outcomes. We make it higher signal and more frequent. Facilitator follow-up converts connections into real outcomes. And every event generates data that makes the next one better. The full methodology, research, and Airtable base are linked in the Google Doc. Happy to discuss any of this. Thanks."
