# Loom Video Script

8 slides. Each section below matches one slide. Arrow keys to navigate.

---

## Slide 1: Title
*Show briefly, start talking.*

"Hi, I'm Eitan. For this work test I designed a curated talent marketplace for AI safety. Let me walk you through what I built and why."

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

"BlueDot has trained over 7,000 people. Some are genuinely exceptional. But there's no fast track to get them into impactful work. 10-30% of AI safety fellowship alumni cycle into another fellowship rather than getting a job or starting a project. Funders like Coefficient Giving gave away over $150M in 2025 and want more good applications. The talent is there. The opportunities are there. They're just not meeting."

---

## Slide 3: The Solution

"The solution is a monthly, invite-only event. 50 people, 3 hours. Two sides of the room: 10-15 institutional leaders, funders, hiring managers, program directors. And 30-35 of BlueDot's highest-rated alumni, invited based on course performance and facilitator recommendations. Ten structured 15-minute 1-on-1s per person, with pre-matching based on profiles."

---

## Slide 4: Why It Works

"We're not reinventing the wheel. EAG events already prove that curated 1-on-1 matching produces career outcomes: about 10 meaningful connections per attendee at $70 per connection. We take that proven mechanic and make it more exclusive, more frequent, monthly instead of a few times a year, and more accountable, with dedicated facilitator follow-up for every participant."

---

## Slide 5: Why BlueDot

"This requires two assets in the same organization. First, a curated talent database: BlueDot has performance data on 7,000+ alumni, enough signal to curate the invite list. Second, demand-side relationships: BlueDot already works with Coefficient Giving, MATS, Apart, Constellation, and hiring managers at safety orgs. Having both is what makes this possible."

---

## Slide 6: The Specific Outcome

"The specific outcome we're measuring: every attendee leaves with at least one concrete opportunity they wouldn't have found otherwise. Job interviews, offers, hires, funding applications, collaborations, all traced back to event connections. And we track this through facilitator follow-up calls, not just surveys. There's evidence from an EAGx Australia study that surveys alone don't capture real impact at 6 months. The facilitator tracking is what makes the measurement credible."

---

## Slide 7: The System
*Switch to Airtable tab briefly here.*

"For Part 2, I built a 5-layer system. Operations through Swapcard and Luma. Data collection through forms. Outcome tracking in a live Airtable base. And a monthly improvement engine. The key is Layer 3: participant tracking. Every attendee gets an assigned facilitator who does monthly calls to convert connections into outcomes. The event alone doesn't produce results. Follow-up does. [Switch to Airtable to show it briefly.]"

---

## Slide 8: My Approach

"My approach was three parts. First, diagnose the problem: where exactly is the talent pipeline breaking? Second, design the solution to match that specific bottleneck, and iterate on it. Third, build the system to measure whether it actually works. All the documentation is in a Google Doc with the full submission, and I also have a GitHub repository with all the research, methodology, and presentation materials. Happy to discuss any of this. Thanks."
