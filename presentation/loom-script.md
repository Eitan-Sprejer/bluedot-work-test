# Loom Video Script

Navigate slides with arrow keys. Each slide accompanies one section below.

---

## Slide 1: Title
*Just show the title slide briefly, start talking immediately.*

"Hi, I'm Eitan. For this work test I designed a curated talent marketplace for AI safety. Let me walk you through what I built and why."

---

## Slide 2: The Problem
"Before picking an event format, I spent most of my time on problem diagnosis. BlueDot has trained over 7,000 people. Some of them are genuinely exceptional. But right now there's no fast track to get those people into impactful work. 10-30% of AI safety fellowship alumni cycle into another fellowship rather than actually getting a job or starting a project. Meanwhile, funders like Coefficient Giving have more money than good applications. The talent is there. The opportunities are there. They're just not meeting."

---

## Slide 3: Where the Pipeline Breaks
"Specifically, the pipeline breaks in four places. Orgs can't find the senior talent they need, even though those people exist in BlueDot's database. Funders want more applications. Training doesn't automatically convert to impact. And institutions in the space don't coordinate enough with each other."

---

## Slide 4: Strategic Clarity
"I think my main contribution here is strategic clarity. I spent time figuring out where the pipeline is actually breaking before designing anything. From my research and from conversations I've had in this space, the core insight is: great talent needs a fast track. BlueDot knows who the top people are. The Community Lead's job is knowing why an event exists, not just how to run one."

---

## Slide 5: The Solution
"So the solution is a monthly, invite-only event. 50 people, 3 hours. Two sides of the room: on one side, 10-15 institutional leaders, funders, hiring managers, program directors. On the other, 30-35 of BlueDot's highest-rated alumni. The curation is the product. Every person you meet is worth your time."

---

## Slide 6: Why BlueDot
"Why can only BlueDot do this? Two assets no one else has. First, a talent database with performance data on 7,000+ people. BlueDot knows who the top 1% are. Second, direct relationships with the demand side: funders, safety orgs, programs. Nobody else can curate both sides of this room."

---

## Slide 7: The Format
"The format is simple. 10 structured 1-on-1s, 15 minutes each. First five are pre-matched based on profiles. Last five are self-selected. Last 20 minutes everyone writes down their concrete next steps. Cost is about $12-28 per attendee, compared to $1,200 at EAG. We're not reinventing the wheel. EAG proves this model works. We just make it higher signal."

---

## Slide 8: The System
"For Part 2, I built a 5-layer system. [show Airtable briefly if possible] The key differentiator is Layer 3: participant tracking. Every attendee gets an assigned facilitator who does monthly calls to help them convert connections into actual outcomes. The event alone doesn't produce results. Follow-up does. I built a live Airtable base with 6 tables that tracks events, next steps, feedback, outcomes, and individual participant progress."

---

## Slide 9: Metrics
"We measure real outcomes, not just satisfaction scores. The north star is job interviews, hires, funding applications, and collaborations, all traced back to event connections through facilitator calls. There's evidence from EAGx Australia that standard surveys alone don't capture real impact. That's why the facilitator tracking matters."

---

## Slide 10: Close
"The talent exists. The opportunities exist. This event connects them. Monthly, curated, outcome-tracked. The only thing like it in SF. I used Claude Code as a research and brainstorming partner for this. The full methodology and research are in the appendices. Happy to discuss any of this. Thanks."

---

**Total time: ~4-5 minutes at normal speaking pace.**

**Tips:**
- Have the presentation open in one browser tab
- Have the Airtable base open in another tab (switch to it briefly during slide 8)
- Don't read the script word-for-word, just glance at the key points
- Speak to the "why" more than the "what"
